Research

My research focuses on advancing the understanding and application of readability assessment in specialized domains—particularly in chemistry and biochemistry—using cutting-edge AI technologies. I leverage Large Language Models like ChatGPT to develop nuanced, context-aware frameworks for evaluating scientific texts and address challenges such as integrating textual and non-textual data.

Key Research Questions and Objectives

Comparative Analysis of Readability Metrics:

How do LLM-based assessments compare to traditional readability formulas in terms of accuracy and comprehensiveness? What limitations of traditional metrics can LLMs overcome?

Reliability and Effectiveness of LLMs:

How reliable are LLMs like ChatGPT in classifying the readability of chemistry texts given domain-specific challenges? What are the implications of LLM biases?

Explainability and Transparency:

How can prompt engineering and feature attribution improve the transparency of AI-driven readability classifications?

Identifying Features of Text Difficulty:

What linguistic and domain-specific factors contribute to text difficulty, and how can these insights tailor educational materials?
