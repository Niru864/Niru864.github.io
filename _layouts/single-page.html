    Research

    My research focuses on advancing the understanding and application of readability assessment in specialized domains, particularly chemistry and biochemistry, using cutting-edge AI technologies. I am particularly interested in leveraging Large Language Models (LLMs) like ChatGPT to develop more nuanced and context-aware frameworks for evaluating the readability of scientific texts. My work addresses the unique challenges posed by domain-specific content, such as the integration of textual and non-textual information (e.g., chemical equations, diagrams, and experimental descriptions), which are critical for conveying complex scientific concepts.

    Key Research Questions and Objectives

    1. Comparative Analysis of Readability Metrics:
      • How do LLM-based readability assessments compare to traditional readability formulas (e.g., Flesch-Kincaid, Dale-Chall) in terms of accuracy and comprehensiveness?
      • What are the limitations of traditional metrics when applied to chemistry and biochemistry texts, and how can LLMs overcome these limitations?
    2. Reliability and Effectiveness of LLMs in Domain-Specific Readability:
      • How reliable and effective are LLMs like ChatGPT in classifying the readability of chemistry-related texts, given the biases and challenges of domain-specific content?
      • What are the implications of LLM biases (e.g., egocentric bias, length bias, order bias) on readability assessment in scientific domains?
    3. Explainability and Transparency in AI-Driven Readability Assessment:
      • How can we improve the transparency and explainability of LLM-driven readability classifications, particularly in specialized domains like chemistry?
      • What is the impact of structured adaptation strategies, such as Chain-of-Thought (CoT) prompting and feature attribution analysis, on LLM performance in readability classification?
    4. Identifying Features of Text Difficulty:
      • What specific linguistic and domain-specific features of chemistry texts contribute to their difficulty, as identified by LLMs?
      • How can these insights be used to tailor educational materials to better meet the needs of students?

    Methodological Approach

    • Prompt Engineering: I explore various prompting techniques, including multi-choice question (MCQ) formats, prompt ordering variations, and explicit rationale requests, to analyze how different strategies affect ChatGPT’s classification accuracy.
    • Post-Hoc Analysis: I incorporate post-hoc analysis techniques to better understand which linguistic and chemical features influence ChatGPT’s readability judgments, ensuring that AI-driven insights align with the complexity and structure of chemistry education.
    • Explainability Techniques: I integrate explainability methods, such as concept-based explanations and feature attribution, to make LLM reasoning processes more transparent and interpretable.

    Broader Impact

    My research contributes to the growing intersection of AI, science education, and interpretability research, addressing the need for trustworthy and transparent AI solutions in domain-specific learning environments. By developing more robust and context-aware readability assessment frameworks, I aim to:

    • Enhance the accessibility and comprehension of scientific texts for students and educators.
    • Provide instructors with tools to select and tailor educational materials that optimize learning outcomes.
    • Advance the field of AI-driven readability assessment by addressing domain-specific challenges and improving the explainability of AI models.

    Future Directions

    • Exploring the application of LLMs to other scientific domains, such as physics and biology, to develop generalized readability assessment frameworks.
    • Investigating the role of multimodal AI models in evaluating the readability of texts that integrate textual, visual, and symbolic information.
    • Developing AI-driven tools for real-time readability assessment and adaptive learning in educational settings.
